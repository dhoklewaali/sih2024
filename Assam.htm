Assam Cultural Heritage Sites

Rang Ghar

Location: Sivasagar district

Coordinates: 26.9817° N, 94.6279° E

Description: Rang Ghar is a two-storied building that once served as a royal sports-pavilion. Built in the 18th century, it's one of the oldest surviving amphitheaters in Asia. The structure showcases unique Ahom architecture with its organic building materials and distinctive roof inspired by an inverted royal Ahom long boat.

Sivasagar

Location: Sivasagar district

Coordinates: 26.9826° N, 94.6425° E

Description: Sivasagar, formerly known as Rangpur, was the capital of the Ahom Kingdom. It's home to numerous historical monuments including the Sivasagar tank, Talatal Ghar, and Shiva Dol. These structures showcase the rich cultural heritage and architectural prowess of the Ahom dynasty.

Deopani Durga Temple

Location: Nalbari district

Coordinates: 26.4416° N, 91.4391° E

Description: The Deopani Durga Temple is an ancient shrine dedicated to Goddess Durga. Known for its unique architecture and spiritual significance, it attracts devotees throughout the year. The temple is particularly famous for its Durga Puja celebrations, showcasing the rich cultural traditions of Assam.

Kareng Ghar

Location: Sivasagar district

Coordinates: 26.9745° N, 94.7510° E

Description: Kareng Ghar, also known as the Garhgaon Palace, was the royal palace of the Ahom kings. Built in the 18th century, it's a prime example of Ahom architecture. The seven-storied structure, made mainly of wood and bricks, showcases the advanced engineering skills of the Ahom period.

Majuli

Location: Majuli district

Coordinates: 26.9520° N, 94.1658° E

Description: Majuli is the world's largest river island and a hub of Neo-Vaishnavite culture. It's known for its Satras (Vaishnavite monasteries), unique pottery, and colorful festivals. The island's rich biodiversity and cultural landscape make it a unique heritage site, showcasing the harmony between nature and human culture.

Madan Kamdev Temple

Location: Kamrup district

Coordinates: 26.2361° N, 91.7468° E

Description: The Madan Kamdev Temple, often referred to as the 'Khajuraho of the East', is an archaeological site featuring ruins of a temple complex. Dating back to the 9th-10th century, it's known for its erotic sculptures and intricate stone carvings. The site offers insights into the artistic and cultural heritage of medieval Assam.
