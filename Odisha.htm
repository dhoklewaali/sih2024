Odisha Cultural Heritage Sites

Ratnagiri

Location: Jajpur district

Coordinates: 20.6397 N, 86.3365 E

Description: Ratnagiri is an ancient Buddhist site in Odisha, known for its monasteries, stupas, and sculptures. Dating back to the 5th century, it was once a prominent center for Buddhist learning and culture. The site is a key part of the Buddhist heritage in India.

Udaygiri

Location: Jajpur district

Coordinates: 20.6360 N, 86.3326 E

Description: Udaygiri, located near Ratnagiri, is another significant Buddhist site. It is home to numerous ancient monasteries and stupas that date back to the Gupta period. The site offers a glimpse into the rich Buddhist heritage of Odisha.

Lalitagiri

Location: Cuttack district

Coordinates: 20.6047 N, 86.3024 E

Description: Lalitagiri is one of the earliest Buddhist complexes in Odisha. It features stupas, monasteries, and a significant collection of Buddhist sculptures. The site is also believed to be home to relics of the Buddha, making it an important pilgrimage site.

Satkosia

Location: Angul district

Coordinates: 20.7394 N, 84.7230 E

Description: Satkosia Gorge is a unique natural heritage site in Odisha, known for its stunning landscape and biodiversity. The gorge, where the Mahanadi River cuts through the Eastern Ghats, is a habitat for a variety of wildlife, including the endangered gharial.

Harishankar

Location: Balangir district

Coordinates: 20.7667 N, 83.5333 E

Description: Harishankar

Description: Harishankar is a well-known pilgrimage site in Odisha, located on the slopes of the Gandhamardhan hills. It is famous for its temples dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Shiva, and the scenic waterfalls nearby. The site is a blend of religious and natural heritage.

Balasore

Location: Balasore district

Coordinates: 21.4927 N, 86.9332 E

Description: Balasore is a historic town in Odisha known for its cultural and maritime heritage. It was an important port during the medieval period and played a key role in trade and commerce. The town is also famous for its temples, especially the Khirachora Gopinath temple.
