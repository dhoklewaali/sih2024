West Bengal Cultural Heritage Sites

Coochbehar Palace

Location: Cooch Behar district

Coordinates: 26.3254° N, 89.4462° E

Description: Cooch Behar Palace, also known as the Victor Jubilee Palace, is a magnificent palace built in 1887 during the reign of Maharaja Nripendra Narayan. The palace is inspired by the Buckingham Palace in London and showcases Italian Renaissance architecture.

Bishnupur

Location: Bankura district

Coordinates: 23.0734° N, 87.3199° E

Description: Bishnupur is famous for its terracotta temples, built during the Malla dynasty in the 17th and 18th centuries. The town is a significant center for traditional crafts and is renowned for its classical music tradition, particularly the Bishnupur Gharana.

Hazaduari Palace

Location: Murshidabad district

Coordinates: 24.1904° N, 88.2706° E

Description: Hazaduari Palace, meaning the "Palace of a Thousand Doors," was built in the early 19th century by Nawab Nazim Humayun Jah. The palace is a marvel of Indo-European architecture and houses a museum with a vast collection of antiques.

Sandakphu

Location: Darjeeling district

Coordinates: 27.1198° N, 88.0232° E

Description: Sandakphu is the highest peak in West Bengal and a popular trekking destination. It offers panoramic views of the world's tallest peaks, including Everest, Kanchenjunga, Lhotse, and Makalu. The trek to Sandakphu is known for its diverse flora and stunning landscapes.

Bawali Rajbari

Location: South 24 Parganas district

Coordinates: 22.3805° N, 88.2545° E

Description: Bawali Rajbari is a 300-year-old mansion that once belonged to the Zamindars of the region. The Rajbari has been restored and converted into a heritage hotel, offering visitors a chance to experience the grandeur of Bengal's aristocratic past.

Gopeswar Mandir

Location: Kalna, Purba Bardhaman district

Coordinates: 23.2194° N, 88.3647° E

Description: Gopeswar Mandir is one of the most famous terracotta temples in West Bengal. It was built during the 18th century and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its intricate terracotta art and sculptures that depict various scenes from Hindu mythology.
