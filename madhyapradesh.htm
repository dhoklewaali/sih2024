Chanderi

Location: Ashoknagar

Coordinates: 24.72 N 78.13 E

Description: It is surrounded by hills southwest of the Betwa River. Chanderi is surrounded by hills, lakes and forests and is spotted with several monuments of the Bundela Rajputs. It is famous for ancient Jain Temples. Chanderi is mentioned by the Persian scholar Alberuni in 1030. Ghiyas ud din Balban captured the city in 1251 for Nasiruddin Mahmud, Sultan of Delhi.

Orchha

Location: Niwari district of Madhya Pradesh

Coordinates: 25.35 N 78.64 E

Description:Orchha is a popular tourist destination in Madhya Pradesh, famous for its historical significance and architectural wonders, including the Orchha Fort, palaces, temples, and chhatris. The fort complex boasts a harmonious blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural styles, creating an awe-inspiring atmosphere. The temple was constructed by the Bundela Rajputs of the kingdom of Orchha during the reign of Mughal emperor Akbar. Its construction was begun by Madhukar Shah and completed by his son, Vir Singh Deo in the 16th century.

Mandu

Location: southwestern Madhya Pradesh state

Coordinates: 22.3340 N, 75.4003 E

Description: Mandu is thought to have been founded in the 6th century ce by an individual named Munjadeva. The site is positioned on a strategic landscape plateau at an elevation of 2000 feet on Vindhya Mountain ranges. It is endowed with very attractive greenery all around with a number of streams, lakes, and ponds interspersed on it. Mandu was a fortress retreat of Raja Bhoj in the 10th century.

Khajuraho

Location: Chhatarpur district

Coordinates: 24.84 N 79.93 E

Description: Khajuraho is renowned for its temples with intricate erotic sculptures. The Khajuraho group of monuments is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and consists of a group of Hindu and Jain temples built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty. These temples are famous for their architectural brilliance and the erotic sculptures that decorate their walls.
