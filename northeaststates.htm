North-Eastern India Cultural Heritage Sites

Unakoti

Location: Kailashahar, Tripura

Coordinates: 24.2971° N, 92.0946° E

Description: Unakoti, meaning "one less than a crore," is an ancient Shaivite pilgrimage site known for its rock-cut sculptures and murals. The site is believed to date back to the 7th-9th centuries and is a significant archaeological and cultural heritage site in the region.

Tawang Monastery

Location: Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh

Coordinates: 27.5860° N, 91.8583° E

Description: Tawang Monastery is the largest monastery in India and the second largest in the world. Founded in the 17th century, it is an important center of Mahayana Buddhism and a cultural hub in the region, surrounded by stunning Himalayan landscapes.

Nartiang Monoliths

Location: West Jaintia Hills, Meghalaya

Coordinates: 25.6071° N, 92.3882° E

Description: The Nartiang Monoliths are a collection of ancient monoliths that date back to the 16th century. These towering stone structures are associated with the Jaintia kings and are considered some of the largest monoliths in the world, reflecting the rich history and culture of the Jaintia people.

Baijnath Temple

Location: Kangra, Himachal Pradesh

Coordinates: 32.0483° N, 76.6485° E

Description: Baijnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is an ancient temple built in the Nagara style of architecture. Dating back to 1204 AD, it is one of the most important pilgrimage sites in the region and is known for its architectural beauty and spiritual significance.

Kangra Fort

Location: Kangra, Himachal Pradesh

Coordinates: 32.1081° N, 76.2601° E

Description: Kangra Fort is one of the oldest forts in India, with a history dating back to the 4th century BC. The fort has witnessed many battles and is an architectural marvel, offering panoramic views of the surrounding valley and a deep insight into the history of the region.

Neelmahal

Location: Agartala, Tripura

Coordinates: 23.5294° N, 91.2731° E

Description: Neelmahal, also known as the "Lake Palace of Tripura," is a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. Built in the middle of the Rudrasagar Lake, Neelmahal served as a summer residence for the royal family of Tripura. Its reflection on the lake, especially at sunset, is a breathtaking sight.

Rang Ghar

Location: Sivasagar, Assam

Coordinates: 26.9805° N, 94.6378° E

Description: Rang Ghar, often referred to as the "Colosseum of the East," is an ancient amphitheater dating back to the Ahom dynasty. Built in the mid-18th century, it is one of the oldest surviving amphitheaters in Asia and is an iconic symbol of Assam's rich cultural heritage.
